In 1786, Luigi Galvani, an Italian medical professor, discovered that a metal knife touching the leg of a dead frog caused violent twitching. He proposed that the frog’s leg must contain electricity. In 1792, Alessandro Volta disagreed. He proposed that the discovery was centered around dissimilar metal of the knife. When moisture came between them, electricity was created. This discovery led Volta to invent the first modern electric battery, a galvanic cell.
