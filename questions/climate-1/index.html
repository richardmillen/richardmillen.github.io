What happens to ice when it’s warmed?


The temperature increases until it reaches 32F (the freezing point of water), then changes state (becomes liquid) and continues to warm only once it’s finished changing state.

From where does Earth get almost all of it’s energy? What form does it take?


The Sun - as visible light - high energy photons, short length electromagnetic waves.

3

How does the Earth lose energy?


Infrared radiation.

4

What is infrared radiation?


Invisible electromagnetic waves. + Slightly longer wave length, lower energy than light. + Radiated from a warm object. + As heat increases, energy emitted becomes visible. + e.g. A fire radiates heat as well as warming the air around it through convection.

What happens to the light energy that reaches Earth?


30% is reflected back.

  • Clouds
  • Ice crystals
  • Small particles
  • Snow & deserts.

20% is absorbed by the atmosphere.

  • Ozone absorbs ultraviolet.

50% reaches the surface.

  • 29% carried into atmosphere from surface.
    • Warm air.
    • Water vapour. (has far greater impact)
    • Radiated.
  • 21% as radiated energy, trapped by greenhouse gases. Re-radiates back toward surface.
    • Water vapour
    • Carbon dioxide.
